Here is a table to help you choose the level:

Level Experience Skills
beginner For this category your dance experience is likely under half a year. Total finished projects in cover dance is less than six. It takes you a couple of months to learn and practice a dance and you rely on teachers and tutorials. Basic coordination and musicality
easy Your experience is under one year and you completed less than 10 projects. You have been on stage or filmed in public a couple of times. You can deal with basic level changes, rhythms that are harder than regular 1 to 8, some quick movements, and have some strength and fluidity. You know how to do waves and basic hip-hop or shuffle footwork.
medium Your experience is about a year and a half and you completed at least 12 projects. You have some filming and stage experience and understand how to convey emotions in dance. It takes about a month to master a choreography for you. You can handle basic floorwork, have a pretty good balance, stamina, hip control, some flexibility and a thorough understanding of angles. For bg dances you will nedd quite some strength, and for gg dances - nice hand control and fluidity. You know basic hip-hop and jazz funk steps.
hard Your experience is over 2 years and you completed at least 20 projects. You have strong stage presence. You can master a dance in under two weeks. Note that some choreos in this level and a pro level do not feature any expert level moves, but they require significant endurance, strength and control. However some skills that can come in handy for this level are: acro stunts, knowing how to engage your torso and head to improve movement. You will likely need to be comfortable with fast changes of tempo, fast changes of levels, advanced floorwork and footwork. Some basics of popping, vogue, breaking, locking are also great for this level.
pro Your experience is over 3 years. You can learn and perform almost any dance in under 24 hours if needed. You are good at creating textures in your movements. You maintain a good balance, even during turns, jumps, level changes. You can handle speed without losing precision or power. You can add groove without going out of sync with other members of your team if you have one. You likely have some solid background in other dancing styles. For some dances you will need basics of waacking, contemporary, tutting, high heels.
